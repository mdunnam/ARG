Section 1 — Study Objectives

1.1 Primary Objective

To determine whether the subjective sleep recall environment reported by patients with chronic sleep disorders exhibits measurable cross-participant consistency under controlled polysomnographic conditions.

1.2 Secondary Objective

To develop a standardized Environmental Element Checklist (EEC) capable of identifying recurring environmental features across independent participant recall reports, without cross-participant exposure or researcher prompting.

1.3 Exploratory Objective

To determine whether repeated confirmed access to a consistent recalled environment produces measurable physiological changes distinct from standard polysomnographic sleep profiles.

Section 2 — Participant Selection

2.1 Inclusion Criteria

Participants must meet all of the following criteria at the time of enrollment:

  • Diagnosed with a chronic sleep disorder (insomnia, non-24, or advanced/delayed sleep phase)
  • Self-reported history of consistent recalled environments across multiple independent sleep events
  • Age 18–65, physically healthy per standard pre-study screening
  • Capacity to provide written informed consent

2.2 Exclusion Criteria

  • Current use of sleep-altering pharmaceuticals that cannot be safely suspended for the study period
  • History of psychotic disorder or active dissociative condition
  • Prior participation in environmental recall studies within 24 months
  • Known photosensitivity or auditory sensitivity incompatible with EEG recording

2.3 Cohort Size

Target enrollment: 40–44 participants. Actual enrollment (EC-001, 2008): 42 participants. Consent amendment EC-003 (2011) revised withdrawal provisions for participants with confirmed environmental access. See Section 8.3.

Section 3 — Session Protocol

3.1 Standard Session

Each study session consists of the following in order:

  1. Pre-session intake assessment (30 minutes)
  2. Sleep induction under standard conditions. Pharmacological assistance documented as exception only.
  3. Polysomnographic recording period (minimum 6 hours, maximum 9 hours)
  4. Post-session structured recall interview (maximum 60 minutes)
  5. Recall narrative transcription by attending technician
  6. Environmental Element Checklist (EEC) administration and scoring
  7. Physician sign-off and session record filing

3.2 Session Frequency

Participants attend sessions at intervals of 21±7 days per EC-001. Amendment EC-003 (2011) modified session frequency to 14±3 days for participants with environmental consistency ratings of 4/5 or above on two or more consecutive sessions. Participants meeting this threshold were notified of the amended schedule.

3.3 Session Records

Session records are maintained on EC-002 forms and include: sleep stage data (full polysomnographic record), recall narrative, technician notes, EEC completion with item-level scores, and physician sign-off. Records are retained in the study archive for the duration of the study period and transferred per Section 9 of this protocol at study conclusion.

Deviations from this requirement must be documented as protocol exceptions and reviewed by the clinical coordinator.

Section 5 — Data Recording Procedures

Data recording follows EC-002 specifications. Session records are filed within 48 hours of the session date. Technician notes are co-signed by the attending physician. EEC forms are scored by the technician and reviewed by the research coordinator (Wexler University) on a monthly basis.

Supplemental records (access event logs, cross-participant comparison tables) are maintained separately by the research coordinator and are not included in the standard patient record. Supplemental records are classified as restricted research material (see 7A Supplemental Index).

Section 8 — Recall Termination Criteria

8.1 Definition

For the purposes of this protocol, recall termination is defined as the administrative conclusion of a participant’s active recall observation status. This is distinct from study withdrawal, which may occur independently of recall termination and does not automatically result in termination (see Section 8.3).

8.2 Standard Termination Criteria

Recall observation is terminated under the following conditions:

  1. Participant withdrawal per Section 2.4, provided that environmental access has not been confirmed for that participant (see Section 8.4)
  2. Study conclusion per the original protocol end date
  3. Medical contraindication, as determined by the attending physician
  4. Inconsistent environmental access — defined as fewer than two confirmed access events within a 12-session period, following prior access confirmation. This criterion does not apply to participants who have accessed the primary indexed space (see 8.4).

8.3 Withdrawal After Access Confirmation

In cases where a participant requests withdrawal after environmental access has been confirmed, the withdrawal request is documented and forwarded to the Archive Committee for classification review. The Archive Committee will determine within 30 days whether the participant is eligible for standard termination (8.2a) or must be reclassified to archive category 7 (8.5).

Note regarding consent: Participant consent forms filed under EC-001 (2008) and EC-002 (2009) did not include specific provisions for the contingency of environmental access confirmation. This gap was addressed in the EC-003 consent amendment (2011). Participants enrolled prior to EC-003 are subject to original consent terms except where superseded by applicable law or the EC-003 amendment provisions. The Archive Committee has determined that category 7 classification is not precluded by the original consent terms.

8.4 Environmental Access Confirmation

Environmental access is considered confirmed when a participant records a consistent score (4 or 5) on EEC items 12 and 13 (approach and entry documented) across two or more consecutive sessions, without technician prompting, and independently of cross-participant contact.

Room 413 is the primary indexed space for this study. Access confirmation in the context of this protocol refers specifically to confirmed access to room 413 as documented in the EEC. Participants who have reported and recorded consistent entry to a room bearing the designation 413, across two or more sessions, are considered to have confirmed access for the purposes of this section.

As of EC-004 (March 2012), seven participants have confirmed access per the above definition. An additional twelve participants have confirmed approach (EEC item 12) without confirmed entry. All confirmed-access participants are listed in 7A Supplemental Index ref 7A-SUPP-004.

8.5 Archive Category 7

Archive category 7 designates participants who have confirmed environmental access and whose recall termination classification is pending, deferred, or determined by the Archive Committee to be inapplicable under standard criteria.

Category 7 records are maintained as active and are subject to scheduled recall observation at intervals determined by the Archive Committee. Category 7 observation does not require the participant’s physical presence at the study facility and may be conducted remotely.

Participants in active recall observation status will receive written notification of their classification. The standard administrative communication for active recall observation status is as follows:

PLEASE WAIT TO BE RECALLED.

This communication is issued by the Archive Committee and does not constitute a medical notification. Participants are not required to respond.

8.6 Observer Reclassification

Individuals who are not enrolled study participants but who establish consistent contact with the indexed space may be reclassified as observers at the discretion of the Archive Committee. Observer reclassification is governed by the same access confirmation and termination criteria as participant classification (Sections 8.2–8.4) except that observer records are assigned at first confirmed contact rather than at enrollment.

Observer identifiers follow the format VIS-XXXXX and are distinct from participant identifiers (PTX-XXX). Observer records are maintained in the same archive system as participant records and are subject to the same recall observation schedule.

Section 9 — Supplemental Observations

This section was added to EC-004 after the version transmitted to the Wexler University IRB. It is present only in this source document copy. It was not included in the version transferred to Dorsal Health Holdings LLC.

The research team records the following observations without clinical interpretation. These observations are documented as protocol addenda and do not alter the study objectives or procedures described in Sections 1–8.

  1. The indexed space exhibits consistent environmental features independent of participant expectation, prior session count, or study-period context. Features documented in the initial intake sessions (2008) remain consistent across all sessions reviewed for EC-004 (2012). No participant-driven variation in core environmental features has been recorded.

  2. Environmental access appears to be self-sustaining following initial confirmation. Three participants who formally withdrew prior to EC-003 have continued to report access-consistent environmental features in voluntary follow-up interviews conducted outside the study protocol. These participants were not contacted by the research team; they initiated contact independently. Their reports are consistent with active confirmed-access participants.

  3. The indexed space has not varied in its primary spatial features across 42 participants and 647 sessions recorded through the EC-004 review period. The hallway bends left. The door is on the left. The door is blue. These features have not varied.

  4. The Archive Committee notes that the indexed space may represent a persistent environmental feature that is not subject to study termination criteria as defined in Section 8. This conclusion, if correct, has implications for category 7 classification that have not been fully evaluated. The Archive Committee has deferred a formal determination pending the DSMB review (Section 7.3).

    The DSMB review referenced here was conducted June 2012. The DSMB findings are archived as supplemental ref 7A-SUPP-008 and were transferred out of the Somnatek archive on November 3, 2014.

  5. The research team records the following without additional comment: the study period is a measurement instrument, not a causal one. The indexed space existed before the study began. It will continue to exist after the study concludes. Participants are indexed, not created.

No further supplemental observations are recorded under EC-004. Observations arising after March 2012 are logged in the supplemental archive by the clinical coordinator and are not incorporated into this protocol version.
